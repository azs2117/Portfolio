legenda :

Legenda is a visual bookmarking tool designed to help users track, organize, and return to saved reading links with clarity and intention. Instead of relying on text-heavy notes apps, users can paste any article URL—whether from Substack, NYT, JSTOR, or other platforms—and generate visual tiles with metadata like save date and estimated read time. With scheduling and reminder features, legenda helps curious readers build intentional reading habits while keeping their digital knowledge library beautifully organized.

The Concept

Legenda—Latin for “something to be read”—is a minimalist reading organizer for curious minds who collect more articles than they can keep up with. I designed this tool for a friend who saves dozens of links across Substack, NYT, JSTOR, and Twitter threads—but struggles to revisit them without context or prioritization. Like many information seekers, she needed more than just a notes app: she needed a way to track, schedule, and return to saved content with intention.

How might we help curious readers organize and return to saved articles in a way that feels visual, intentional, and manageable?

Design Strategy

The core feature is simple: paste a link and legenda pulls in the title, preview image, and metadata to generate an interactive card. From there, users can organize articles by urgency or theme, and even set reading reminders to build a more intentional reading habit. The interface is clean, tactile, and built for thinkers who toggle between browser tabs and personal goals.

Next Steps

In future iterations, legenda will support curation boards for thematic reading (e.g. health, politics, design) and social sharing—turning saved tabs into shared libraries. For now, it’s a focused prototype built in a day, with plans to move into development next.
