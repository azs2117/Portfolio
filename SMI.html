Subscription Management Interface Redesign

Checkout the live experience here.

The Subscription Management Interface (SMI) Redesign improved the way customers track, modify, and manage their subscriptions by shifting from a single-page list to a structured multi-page experience. The new design introduced a Subscription Management Page for an organized view of all subscriptions and a Subscription Detail Page with clear order status updates and enhanced modification options. By addressing long-standing usability frustrations, this redesign provided customers with greater control and clarity over their recurring orders.

Role

Product Designer & Product Manager

Timeline

3 months
May - August 2023

Skills + Tools

Prototyping, Competitive Research, Interaction Design, Figma, A/B Testing

The Challenge

The previous interface displayed all subscriptions in a single-page list, making it difficult for users to track shipments, modify orders, or understand their subscription status. Customers frequently contacted support due to unclear cancellation flows and limited product modification options. The redesign needed to improve organization and navigation while ensuring that users could easily update, track, and adjust their subscriptions with minimal confusion.

The Approach

To create a more intuitive experience, I conducted competitive research on industry best practices and worked closely with developers to translate complex subscription logic into a multi-page system. The redesign introduced a structured Subscription Management Page with clear shipment details and a Subscription Detail Page with new modification features, such as swapping products and one-time removals. By designing flexible user flows for different order statuses—Upcoming, Processing, and In-Transit—we ensured that customers had the right options available at the right time.

Results & Impact

The redesigned interface significantly reduced customer support inquiries related to subscription management, as users could now clearly track and modify their orders. Enhanced organization and navigation led to increased customer confidence in managing their subscriptions, improving overall retention and satisfaction. Through this project, I learned the importance of early collaboration with developers to ensure feasibility and how structured design decisions can directly reduce friction in user interactions.
