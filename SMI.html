Subscription Management Interface Redesign

Check out the live experience here.


The Subscription Management Interface (SMI) Redesign improved the way customers track, modify, and manage their subscriptions by shifting from a single-page list to a structured multi-page experience. The new design introduced a Subscription Management Page for an organized view of all subscriptions and a Subscription Detail Page with clear order status updates and enhanced modification options. By addressing long-standing usability frustrations, this redesign provided customers with greater control and clarity over their recurring orders.

Role

Product Designer & Product Manager

Timeline

3 months
May - August 2023

Skills + Tools

Figma, Prototyping, Competitive Research

The Challenge

Bonafide Health, a women’s health company offering non-hormonal menopause supplements, needed a more intuitive way for customers to manage recurring subscriptions. The original Subscription Management Interface (SMI) was a single-page list where all orders—past, current, and future—were displayed together. This made it difficult for customers to track upcoming shipments or modify their subscriptions with confidence. Our challenge was clear:

How might we simplify subscription management to reduce support inquiries and improve customer confidence?

Research & Insights

Customer experience (CX) feedback revealed two major pain points: confusion between canceling a single product and canceling the entire subscription, and lack of clarity around the status of active orders. Support tickets frequently stemmed from misunderstandings around shipment timelines or modification options. To better understand best practices, I conducted a competitive analysis and found that most modern interfaces used a multi-page system with a visual overview and separate detail views for each subscription.

Design Strategy

Guided by this insight, I designed a new multi-page interface composed of a Subscription Overview Page (SOP) and dedicated Subscription Detail Pages (SDPs). The SOP displayed each subscription as an easy-to-read tile with key information like next shipment date, product list, frequency, and total cost. Each tile served as an entry point to its corresponding detail page. On the SDP, I organized functionality into three main sections: subscription tracking, order modification, and product details. To reduce user drop-off, I introduced in-context product previews, a "Cancel One Time" button to clarify temporary removals, and direct actions like “Swap” and “Add Product” to streamline common tasks.

Outcome & Impact

The redesign resulted in a significant drop in customer support inquiries related to subscription issues. Usability improved across both desktop and mobile views, and internal user testing showed higher success rates for key tasks like editing orders or canceling individual items. This project reinforced the value of early collaboration with engineering to ensure feasibility and revealed how small UX changes—when grounded in real customer pain points—can meaningfully reduce friction and increase retention.
